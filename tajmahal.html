The Taj Mahal: A Monument of Love and Beauty

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most iconic and beautiful structures in the world. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. The construction began around 1632 and was completed in 1653, involving over 20,000 workers and artisans.

Architectural Marvel
The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning Mughal architecture, which is a blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles. The main structure is made of white marble, inlaid with semi-precious stones, and features intricate carvings and calligraphy. The complex also includes a mosque, a guest house, and beautiful gardens.

Symbol of Eternal Love
The Taj Mahal is often referred to as a symbol of eternal love, as it was built out of Shah Jahan's deep love and grief for Mumtaz Mahal. The monument's beauty and symmetry have made it one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

UNESCO World Heritage Site
In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for being "the jewel of Islamic art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage".

Visiting the Taj Mahal
Today, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors each year who come to marvel at its beauty and learn about its history. The monument is particularly enchanting during sunrise and sunset when the marble appears to change color.
